## Verdict
- **Does tradable expansion score improve over raw burst score?** No. The best raw target-3 baseline (`BASE_ATM100_SCORE5_P80_300_SPREAD2_DEPTH250`) made `11,525` vs the best TES target-3 variant (`TES_ATM100_SCORE5_TES6`) at `9,600`. On the best-TES ablation, removing TES improved PnL further.
- **Does ATM±200 add value or dilute edge?** It dilutes edge in this sample. `TES_ATM100_SCORE5_TES6` finished at `9,600`, while `TES_ATM200_SCORE5_TES6` fell to `2,825`; `TES_ATM100_SCORE5_TES8` made `8,250`, while `TES_ATM200_SCORE5_TES8` made `2,200`.
- **Does spread compression matter?** Not in a measurable way here. Removing the spread-compression requirement changed PnL by `0`.
- **Does adjacent strike confirmation matter?** Not yet. Removing adjacent confirmation changed PnL by `0`.
- **Does opposite-side suppression matter?** Not yet. Removing opposite-side suppression changed PnL by `0`.
- **Does confirmation-before-entry reduce fake bursts?** It reduces trade count, but not enough to improve edge. `CONFIRM_1S_ATM100_SCORE5_TES6` made `1,950` on `19` trades vs `TES_ATM100_SCORE5_TES6` at `9,600` on `73` trades.
- **Is target=3 edge positive without relying on 7-point promotion?** Yes, but it is mostly the old raw baseline. `BASE_ATM100_SCORE5_P80_300_SPREAD2_DEPTH250` made `11,525` with PF `1.39`.
- **Does any variant beat actual live system, previous near-ATM baseline, and broad burst replay?** Against the live system: yes. Against the previous near-ATM baseline: no; the prior best (`ATM100_SCORE5_P80_300_SPREAD2_DEPTH250`) made `156,550`, far above this TES study. Against the broad burst replay family: yes; the best broad variant (`ranked_score5_only`) was `-301,343`.
- **Is any TES variant live-patch ready?** No. Research promising but not live-ready.
- **Exact next candidate to test:** `TES_ATM100_SCORE5_TES6`, but research-only and directly benchmarked against `BASE_ATM100_SCORE5_P80_300_SPREAD2_DEPTH250`. If TES cannot beat that raw baseline over the next 5-10 tape days, kill the TES layer.

## Caveats
- This is diagnostic replay, not broker-grade execution simulation.
- Candidate features reuse prior burst-onset outputs for cached dates and raw fallback compute for missing dates.
- Cross-strike confirmation/suppression is computed from simultaneous burst-onset events, not continuous raw score state across the full lattice.
- Replay uses fixed quantity 500 for comparability across variants; actual live trades used real runtime sizing.
